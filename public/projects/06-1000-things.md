over the years the icon system has gone through a couple of phases, adapting to typeface changes, system tweaks, and collaborative brand efforts:

+ an audit + overhaul to establish the foundation
+ a refresh for the genesis of the new arcade design system
+ a restyling to pair with a shiny new typeface

<details class="project-collapse">
<summary><h2>the audit / system genesis <img class="collapse-indicator" src="/media/icons-refresh/icon-svgs/Plus.svg" alt="" /></h2></summary>
<div class="collapse-content">

two years into my time as a product designer at cash app, I pitched an icon and asset audit noting the gaps, the redundancies, and the plan, thus becoming a founding member of our Systems & Standards team.

the audit and initial icon overhaul phase happened alongside the genesis of arcade, our design system. this phase was mostly concerned with defining consistent shapes, strokes, beziers and overall construction.

in tandem, my teammates and I took this opportunity to streamline the retrieval pipeline so that icon updates could easily be fetched from figma by our engineering partners (~2021; see also icons-mcp for its 2026 form).

</div></details>

<div class="collapse-content">

the icon system deserved another redo alongside the next launch of arcade - a launch fully equipped with new type styling and foundational components, we took the opportunity to update stale metaphors and reduce the chunky stroke weight to improve visual balance on core screens.

</div></details>

## the restyling

with the launch of a new typeface came a major update to the icon set. by this point, hundreds of icons had been added into the library, and the icons were soon to be marked for use across all Block brands in a unified, federated system.

icons and type carry equal weight in a visual language. side by side, they should feel like they belong together. cash sans is the reference point, setting the standard for line quality across our iconography.
