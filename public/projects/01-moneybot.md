_giving an AI financial assistant a face_

moneybot is cash app's AI-powered personal financial assistant - it helps users manage, track, and optimize their money


### part I: the message
the year was 2025. in a small house on a hill in westchester, new york (which some people would call "upstate" but it's not really upstate) a woman's phone buzzed. a message from the tower:

```build a glyph. a mark. a symbol the people will recognize and turn to when things get tricky. warm but not soft. sharp but not unapproachable. the people need to trust it with their money, which is to say, the people need to trust it with their lives. you have 72 hours.
```

she read it twice and closed the message. it had begun - they had been building toward this for moons and she knew she'd be the one called at the final transmission.

she understood glyphs - she had created a thousand of them. she got to work.

she assembled glyph after glyph after glyph, each batch transmitted back to the tower, batches to be rendered before the <span class="hover-icon" data-icon="/media/moneybot/jack.png">commander</span> himself.

<img src="/media/moneybot/glyphs-intro.png" alt="glyphs intro" />
> I lost a lot of work after the <span class="hover-icon" data-icon="/media/moneybot/nyt.png" data-icon-width="200px">thanos snap</span> but just pretend there are dozens of impressive iterations here

the cycle was always the same: she tinkered. she transmitted. the tower received. the coordinates shifted. she tinkered again.

her unit ran the relay. while she was building, they were the ones carrying each batch to the commander, holding formation in the review, filtering the noise on the return signal so she could stay locked in. every round, every batch, they kept the frequency clean so she didn't have to leave the lab.

they translated the latest message:

```moneybot should feel alive.```

_a glyph for a machine. a machine the people would trust with their money._ the weight of that kept compounding with every shifted coordinate. she needed to get out of the cycle. she needed to visit the place where humans kept their thoughts before the overtaking. she needed to get to the archives.

### part II: the archives

she transported herself to the archives in the humming wagon of scandinavian integrity. she walked in, smelled the aging paper, and ran her hand along the spines. somewhere in here was the exact moment a human gave a machine its first marks. its first symbols. she needed to stand in that doorway. just for a moment. just long enough to feel a spark.

she looked for the people who came before her, before the technology. jenson, garamond, dreyfuss. susan kare taught a computer to smile with 32 pixels. _32._ and just like that, sparks.

### part III: the lab

she got back in the humming wagon of scandinavian integrity and went straight into the lab. she did not eat. she did not sleep. she created glyph after glyph and transmitted them in batches back to the tower.

she constructed a character. she made another. she assembled expression after expression - receiving updated coordinates with every round. some were too nostalgic. some were too robotic. too spacey, too cubic, too on-the-nose.

by the 800th she could not see straight. 800 will do that to a person. she loaded them into a single grid — 40 across, 20 deep — and transmitted the message. somewhere in the grid was the one.

<img style="border-radius:0" src="/media/moneybot/800-glyphs.png" alt="800" />

she stepped back. she ran the protocol she always ran when the signal inside got noisy: stop transmitting. stop receiving. stand still and let the grid look back at you.

nothing.

she killed the display and went to find sustenance. when she returned, her unit was already there. one of her mates pointed to a quiet section in the corner of the grid. one she had barely rendered twice. "that one," he said. "that's the one."

<img style="border-radius:0" src="/media/moneybot/moneybot-grid.png" alt="moneybot spec" />

she didn't fight it. not because she didn't have doubts. but because she trusted them. the kind of trust you only learn when the mission is short and the coordinates won't stop moving.

she rendered it to final specs. it looked back at her. she transmitted, and for the first time since the mission began, the coordinates held.

and then, on every screen the tower could reach, the moneybot opened its eyes.
